What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances, such as dust, pollen, soot, smoke, and liquid droplets, are introduced into Earth's atmosphere. It poses serious health risks, particularly to vulnerable groups like children, elderly individuals, and those with pre-existing health conditions. The primary contributors to air pollution include transportation, industrial activities, deforestation, and agriculture.

Effects of Air Pollution

Exposure to polluted air can have immediate and long-term effects on health. Short-term exposure can cause eye irritation, coughing, and aggravated asthma, while long-term exposure can increase the risk of heart disease, respiratory illnesses, and even premature death.

Steps to Reduce Air Pollution

  • Reduce the use of fossil fuels by adopting renewable energy sources.
  • Encourage the use of public transportation and electric vehicles.
  • Promote sustainable farming practices and reduce deforestation.
  • Advocate for stronger government policies and regulations on industrial emissions.
  • Increase awareness about the importance of air quality in public health.
